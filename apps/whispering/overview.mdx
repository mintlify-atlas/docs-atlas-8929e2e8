## What is Whispering?

Whispering is designed for quick transcriptions, not long recordings. It's built with transparency and data control in mind:

- **Open-source and transparent**: Your data is stored locally on your device
- **No middleman**: Audio goes directly from your machine to your chosen provider
- **Local-first**: Use Whisper C++ for completely offline, private transcription
- **Cloud options**: Or use Groq, OpenAI, ElevenLabs for fast, accurate results
- **Cost-effective**: Pay providers directly, not subscription fees

<Note>
The terms **Epicenter Whispering** and **Whispering** are interchangeable and refer to the same app.
</Note>

## Use Cases

Whispering is perfect for:

- **Coding**: Dictate code comments, documentation, or commit messages
- **Writing**: Draft emails, documents, or notes hands-free
- **Thinking out loud**: Capture ideas while moving around
- **Accessibility**: Hands-free text input for those who need it
- **Quick notes**: Transcribe thoughts without breaking flow

<Tip>
Whispering is designed for quick transcriptions, not long recordings. For extended recording sessions, use a dedicated recording app.
</Tip>

## Platform Support

Whispering runs on:

- **macOS**: Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3/M4) and Intel
- **Windows**: 64-bit Windows 10 and later
- **Linux**: Ubuntu, Debian, Fedora, and other distributions
- **Web**: Any modern browser at [whispering.epicenterhq.com](https://whispering.epicenterhq.com)

## Open Source Philosophy

Whispering is open-source because fundamental tools shouldn't require trusting a black box. Companies pivot, get acquired, or shut down. But open source is forever.

The codebase is well-documented and designed to be understood and audited. You know where your audio goes, how it's processed, and what data is stored.
